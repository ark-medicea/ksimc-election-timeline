📝 Notes

  • "Clear days" (Cl. 20.1) — Excludes both the day of notice and the day of the event. So "21 clear days" means at least 23 calendar days between sending and the meeting; "4 clear days" means at least 6 calendar days.
  • SGM justification (Cl. 9) — An SGM requires the EC to state that a matter of great importance and urgency must be discussed and cannot await the usual EGM timeline. The justification: Electoral Commissioners were not elected at the AGM on 27 Jul 2025 as required by R. 7.9, and the election cycle cannot begin without them. Routes A and C avoid this by using the standard EGM process.
  • Route C advantage — By pushing the AGM back one week to Sun 14 Jun (still within the 1 Jun – 31 Jul constitutional window), Route C avoids needing an SGM entirely. Standard EGM process, full 21 clear days' notice, no justification debate. Cleanest constitutional path.
  • AGM date constraints (Cl. 6.8) — Must be between 1 June and 31 July. Must not fall on a UK public holiday (or the Friday/Monday either side), or on an auspicious Shia Ithna-Asheri date. Both Sun 7 Jun and Sun 14 Jun 2026 appear clear.
  • Election must be on a Sunday (Cl. 10.1) — Polling hours 10:30–19:30, at least 2 weeks before the AGM.
  • Uncontested positions (R. 7.4.2) — Declared elected without a vote.
  • Contested President (R. 7.4.3) — If more than one candidate, hustings are mandatory (at least 1 day before Election).
  • If no Electoral Commissioners are elected at the EGM/SGM, the entire election cycle cannot proceed.
