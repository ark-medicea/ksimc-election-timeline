⚖️ Route A vs Route B — Comparison

  • Route A (EGM) — Standard procedure. 21 clear days' notice (Cl. 8.1). Requires notice by ~22 Mar to meet the timeline. Commissioners elected Sun 12 Apr. Zero slack remaining.
  • Route B (SGM) — Accelerated under Cl. 9. Only 4 clear days' notice. Notice goes Mon 30 Mar, SGM held Sun 5 Apr. Commissioners elected 1 week earlier than Route A, giving extra buffer for the commission to organise. Requires the EC to justify urgency (see below).
  • Downstream dates are identical — Both routes lead to the same Election (Sun 24 May) and AGM (Sun 7 Jun). The only difference is how quickly the Electoral Commission is stood up.

📝 Notes

  • "Clear days" (Cl. 20.1) — Excludes both the day of notice and the day of the event. So "21 clear days" means at least 23 calendar days between sending and the meeting; "4 clear days" means at least 6 calendar days.
  • SGM justification (Cl. 9) — An SGM requires the EC to state that a matter of great importance and urgency must be discussed and cannot await the usual EGM timeline. The justification here: Electoral Commissioners were not elected at the AGM on 27 Jul 2025 as required by R. 7.9, and the election cycle cannot begin without them. With the AGM constitutionally required between 1 June and 31 July, there is insufficient time for a standard EGM.
  • AGM date constraints (Cl. 6.8) — Must be between 1 June and 31 July. Must not fall on a UK public holiday (or the Friday/Monday either side), or on an auspicious Shia Ithna-Asheri date. Sunday 7 June 2026 appears clear.
  • Election must be on a Sunday (Cl. 10.1) — Polling hours 10:30–19:30, at least 2 weeks before the AGM.
  • Uncontested positions (R. 7.4.2) — Declared elected without a vote.
  • Contested President (R. 7.4.3) — If more than one candidate, hustings are mandatory (at least 1 day before Election).
  • If no Electoral Commissioners are elected at the EGM/SGM, the entire election cycle cannot proceed.
