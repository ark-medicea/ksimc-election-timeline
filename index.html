📨 24-Hour Email Receipt Rule
Notice sent by email is deemed received 24 hours after sending (Cl. 17.3.1). "Clear days" exclude both the day of receipt and the day of the event (Cl. 20.1). Therefore, for X clear days' notice, you must send the notice X+2 calendar days before the event:
• 21 clear days → send 23 calendar days before
• 15 clear days → send 17 calendar days before
• 14 clear days → send 16 calendar days before
• 10 clear days → send 12 calendar days before
• 7 clear days → send 9 calendar days before
• 4 clear days (SGM) → send 6 calendar days before
All dates in the timelines above incorporate this rule.

📝 Notes

  • Route A advantage — By pushing the AGM to Sun 14 Jun (still within the 1 Jun – 31 Jul constitutional window), Route A uses standard EGM process with comfortable slack. No SGM justification debate. Cleanest constitutional path.
  • Route B risk — Same EGM process as Route A but targeting AGM 7 Jun. The late start compresses the timeline. Commission has barely a week between formation and needing to process nominations. Consider holding EGM on a weekday (Wed 22 Apr) rather than waiting for Sunday.
  • SGM justification (Cl. 9) — An SGM requires the EC to state that a matter of great importance and urgency must be discussed and cannot await the usual EGM timeline. The justification: Electoral Commissioners were not elected at the AGM on 27 Jul 2025 as required by R. 7.9, and the election cycle cannot begin without them. Only Route C requires this.
  • AGM date constraints (Cl. 6.8) — Must be between 1 June and 31 July. Must not fall on a UK public holiday (or the Friday/Monday either side), or on an auspicious Shia Ithna-Asheri date. Both Sun 7 Jun and Sun 14 Jun 2026 appear clear.
  • Constitutional amendments (Cl. 18.1, 18.3) — Members must submit amendments to the Hon Sec at least 6 weeks before the AGM. Amendments must then be circulated to members with at least 21 clear days' notice before the meeting.
  • Election must be on a Sunday (Cl. 10.1) — Polling hours 10:30–19:30, at least 2 weeks before the AGM.
  • Uncontested positions (R. 7.4.2) — Declared elected without a vote.
  • Contested President (R. 7.4.3) — If more than one candidate, hustings are mandatory (at least 1 day before Election).
  • If no Electoral Commissioners are elected at the EGM/SGM, the entire election cycle cannot proceed.
