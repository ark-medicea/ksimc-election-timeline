📨 24-Hour Email Receipt Rule
Notice sent by email is deemed received 24 hours after sending (Cl. 17.3.1). "Clear days" exclude both the day of receipt and the day of the event (Cl. 20.1). Therefore, for X clear days' notice, you must send the notice X+2 calendar days before the event:
• 21 clear days → send 23 calendar days before
• 15 clear days → send 17 calendar days before
• 14 clear days → send 16 calendar days before
• 10 clear days → send 12 calendar days before
• 7 clear days → send 9 calendar days before
• 4 clear days (SGM) → send 6 calendar days before
All dates in the timelines above incorporate this rule.

🔄 What Changed vs March Timeline

  • Start date shifted: The original timeline assumed notices sent Mon 30 Mar. That date has passed. All routes now recalculate from Wed 8 April 2026 (today).
  • Original Route C (SGM → Sun 5 Apr) is no longer possible — the SGM date has passed. The SGM route (now Route 1) targets the earliest possible date from today: Tue 14 Apr or Sun 20 Apr.
  • Original Route B (EGM → Wed 22 Apr) is no longer possible — the 21 clear days now expire Thu 30 Apr, pushing the earliest EGM to Fri 1 May.
  • Route 2 (EGM → AGM 7 Jun) is now HIGH RISK: With EGM no earlier than Fri 1 May, the commission has only 3–6 days before register and nominations deadlines. Extremely compressed.
  • Route 3 (Recommended) preserves slack by targeting AGM Sun 14 Jun instead of 7 Jun, giving the commission 10+ days to organise after the EGM. Same structure as original Route A.
  • Route numbers reordered: Routes are now numbered 1 (fastest/SGM), 2 (tight/EGM), 3 (recommended/EGM) for clarity. The original used A, B, C.

📝 Notes

  • Route 3 advantage — By pushing the AGM to Sun 14 Jun (still within the 1 Jun – 31 Jul constitutional window), Route 3 uses standard EGM process with comfortable slack. No SGM justification debate. Cleanest constitutional path.
  • Route 2 risk — Same EGM process as Route 3 but targeting AGM 7 Jun. The commission has barely any time between formation and needing to process nominations. If EGM is held on Sun 4 May (Sunday), the register is due in 3 days. Consider holding EGM on Fri 1 May (weekday) to gain 3 extra days.
  • Route 1 (SGM) advantage — Commissioners in place by mid-April, giving them weeks to prepare. The trade-off: requires an SGM justification that may face scrutiny.
  • SGM justification (Cl. 9) — "Electoral Commissioners were not elected at the AGM on 27 Jul 2025 as required by R. 7.9, and the election cycle cannot begin without them." Only Route 1 requires this.
  • AGM date constraints (Cl. 6.8) — Must be between 1 June and 31 July. Must not fall on a UK public holiday (or the Friday/Monday either side), or on an auspicious Shia Ithna-Asheri date. Both Sun 7 Jun and Sun 14 Jun 2026 appear clear.
  • EGM/SGM can be on a weekday — Only the EC election must be on a Sunday (Cl. 10.1). The EGM/SGM to elect commissioners can be held on any day.
  • Constitutional amendments (Cl. 18.1, 18.3) — Members must submit amendments to the Hon Sec at least 6 weeks before the AGM. Amendments must then be circulated to members with at least 21 clear days' notice before the meeting.
  • Election must be on a Sunday (Cl. 10.1) — Polling hours 10:30–19:30, at least 2 weeks before the AGM.
  • Uncontested positions (R. 7.4.2) — Declared elected without a vote.
  • Contested President (R. 7.4.3) — If more than one candidate, hustings are mandatory (at least 1 day before Election).
  • If no Electoral Commissioners are elected at the EGM/SGM, the entire election cycle cannot proceed.
